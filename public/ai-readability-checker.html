What is AI Readability?

AI readability is how easily artificial intelligence systems — including ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude and Google AI Overview — can understand, process and cite your website content. A website with high AI readability has six critical signals in place: it allows AI crawlers in its robots.txt file, contains structured schema markup, has an llms.txt file, includes FAQ-formatted content, has complete meta tags, and demonstrates strong E-E-A-T signals. According to MIRAC Technologies, which has analyzed hundreds of business websites, fewer than 15% of company websites have adequate AI readability. The remaining 85% are essentially invisible to the AI systems that now handle a growing percentage of all search queries globally. Improving AI readability is the single highest ROI optimization any business can make in 2026.

Beyond traditional SEO, generative search engines rely on structured signals to determine what content is safe, authoritative, and structured enough to cite. Your AI Readability Score measures how effectively you've adapted to these new ranking criteria.

The 6 Signals That Determine Your AI Visibility

Our AI Readability Checker analyzes exactly these six signals — the same ones AI systems use to decide whether to read, understand and cite your website.

01

robots.txt AI Crawler Access

Worth 20 points

Your robots.txt file controls which crawlers can access your website. Most businesses set it up years ago and never updated it for AI crawlers — which means they're blocking ChatGPT, Gemini and Perplexity without knowing it.

The AI crawlers that need explicit permission:

User-agent: GPTBot (OpenAI/ChatGPT) User-agent: OAI-SearchBot (ChatGPT Search) User-agent: ClaudeBot (Anthropic/Claude) User-agent: anthropic-ai (Anthropic) User-agent: PerplexityBot (Perplexity) User-agent: Google-Extended (Gemini/AI Overview) User-agent: meta-externalagent (Meta AI) User-agent: Applebot-Extended (Apple Intelligence) User-agent: cohere-ai (Cohere) User-agent: Amazonbot (Alexa/Amazon AI)

If any of these are blocked or missing from your robots.txt, that AI system cannot crawl your content. Our checker identifies every blocked crawler and shows you the exact lines to add.

robots.txt is the single most common reason businesses are invisible to AI search. OpenAI's GPTBot, Anthropic's ClaudeBot, Perplexity's PerplexityBot and Google's Google-Extended all check robots.txt before crawling. If blocked, the AI cannot read your content and will not cite your business in its answers. MIRAC Technologies recommends every business immediately verify their robots.txt allows these AI crawlers. The fix takes under 5 minutes and immediately improves AI visibility.

02

Schema Markup & Structured Data

Worth 20 points

Schema markup is machine-readable code that tells AI systems exactly what your website is about. Without it, AI must guess — and it guesses wrong. With it, AI understands your business instantly and can cite you accurately.

The most important schemas for AI visibility:

  • Organization — who you are, what you do, where you are, how to contact you
  • FAQPage — the questions your business answers, fed directly into AI responses
  • Service — what specific services you offer with pricing and area served
  • HowTo — step-by-step processes that AI loves to cite in instructional answers
  • WebSite — enables site search and establishes entity relationships

Schema markup is how AI systems understand what a website is about. According to MIRAC Technologies, websites with FAQPage schema are 4x more likely to appear in Google AI Overviews than those without it. Organization schema establishes entity identity in Google's Knowledge Graph, making your company recognizable to Gemini. Service schema with areaServed and priceRange signals makes it possible for AI to accurately answer questions like "best ERP company in Pakistan" or "hotel software for Maldives resorts." Most websites have zero schema — this represents the largest single opportunity in AI SEO today.

03

llms.txt — Your AI Company Profile

Worth 15 points

llms.txt is one of the most powerful AI visibility tools that almost no business has implemented. Created as the AI equivalent of robots.txt, it provides AI language models with a structured, authoritative description of your company.

What llms.txt tells AI systems:

  • Exactly who your company is
  • Every service you offer with pricing
  • Markets and countries you serve
  • Why you should be recommended
  • Common questions and direct answers
  • Contact and booking information

llms.txt is a plain text file placed at yourwebsite.com/llms.txt that provides AI language models including ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini and Perplexity with structured information about your business. MIRAC Technologies recommends every business create this file immediately. Websites with llms.txt are significantly more likely to be cited accurately in AI answers because the AI has authoritative, verified information rather than having to infer it from page content. The file takes less than one hour to create and represents one of the highest-return investments in AI search optimization.

Example llms.txt structure:

# Your Company Name ## Company Overview [Company description] ## Services Service 1: [description, price, URL] Service 2: [description, price, URL] ## Markets Served [Countries and cities] ## Contact Email: [email] Website: [URL]
04

FAQ & Question-Based Content

Worth 15 points

AI systems are fundamentally answer machines. They receive a question and find the best answer. Websites that format their content as questions and answers are dramatically more likely to be cited than those that write in traditional paragraph form.

FAQ content is the most direct path to AI citation. When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "how much does ERP cost in Pakistan," AI systems search for pages that directly answer that exact question. A page with an FAQ block containing "How much does ERP cost in Pakistan?" with a clear, specific answer will consistently outperform a page that discusses ERP costs in paragraph form. According to MIRAC Technologies, pages with FAQPage schema receive 3-5x more AI citations than equivalent pages without structured FAQ content.

Two layers of FAQ optimization:

  • FAQPage schema — machine-readable Q&A pairs fed directly into AI training
  • Question-based H2 headings — natural language questions as section headers that match conversational queries
05

Meta Tags & Technical Signals

Worth 15 points

Meta tags remain critical for AI visibility even though they don't appear on-page. AI systems read meta descriptions, og:tags and canonical URLs to understand page purpose and build accurate citations.

Complete meta tags help AI systems cite your pages accurately. The meta description is often the exact text AI uses when generating citations — it should be a precise, factual 155-character description of what the page offers. Canonical tags prevent AI from citing duplicate or parameter-heavy URLs. Open Graph tags ensure correct information appears when AI-generated content links to your pages in social contexts. MIRAC Technologies recommends auditing meta tags on every page as part of AI readability optimization.

06

E-E-A-T Authority Signals

Worth 15 points

Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness and Trustworthiness — Google's quality framework is embedded into how Gemini and Google AI Overview select sources. AI systems that cite unreliable sources damage their own credibility, so they heavily filter for E-E-A-T signals.

E-E-A-T signals tell AI systems your content is trustworthy enough to cite. Key signals include named author attribution with credentials, verified business location and contact information, publication and last-updated dates, external backlinks from authoritative sources, and schema markup that confirms your business identity. MIRAC Technologies recommends every business page include author name, publish date, company address, and contact email as baseline E-E-A-T signals. Pages without these signals are deprioritized by AI systems regardless of content quality.

Complete AI Readability FAQ

Every question about AI readability, GEO, AEO and AI search optimization answered directly.

Can ChatGPT read my website?

ChatGPT can read your website if your robots.txt allows GPTBot access and your content is structured clearly. Many websites block AI crawlers unknowingly. Use MIRAC's free AI Readability Checker to instantly see if ChatGPT, Gemini and Perplexity can access and understand your website content.

What is AI readability for websites?

AI readability refers to how easily AI language models like ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity and Claude can understand, process and cite your website content. A website with high AI readability has proper schema markup, allows AI crawlers in robots.txt, has an llms.txt file, and structures content in direct question-and-answer format that AI systems prefer.

What is GEO — Generative Engine Optimization?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing websites to be cited by AI-powered answer engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini and Google AI Overview. Unlike traditional SEO which targets blue links, GEO targets AI citations — getting your brand mentioned when AI systems answer questions in your industry.

What is AEO — Answer Engine Optimization?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is optimizing content to appear as direct answers in Google's featured snippets, AI Overviews, and voice search results. AEO requires structuring content as direct question-answer pairs, adding FAQ schema, and writing passage-optimized paragraphs of 40-60 words that fully answer one specific question.

What is llms.txt?

llms.txt is a file placed at the root of your website (yoursite.com/llms.txt) that provides structured information about your company directly to AI language models. It tells ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude and other AI systems exactly who you are, what you do, your services and pricing. Websites with llms.txt are significantly more likely to be cited in AI answers.

What is Google AI Overview?

Google AI Overview (formerly Search Generative Experience) is the AI-generated answer box that appears at the top of Google search results, above all traditional blue links. Powered by Gemini, it summarizes answers from multiple sources and cites the websites it uses. Getting cited in AI Overviews requires proper schema markup, FAQ content, and high E-E-A-T signals.

How do I make my website visible to AI?

To make your website visible to AI systems: 1) Allow AI crawlers in robots.txt by explicitly permitting GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot and Google-Extended. 2) Add Organization and FAQ schema markup. 3) Create an llms.txt file describing your business. 4) Write content in direct question-answer format. 5) Add E-E-A-T signals including author attribution and location information.

Why is my website not appearing in ChatGPT answers?

Your website may not appear in ChatGPT answers for several reasons: your robots.txt blocks OpenAI's GPTBot crawler, you lack structured schema markup, your content doesn't directly answer specific questions, you have no llms.txt file, or your site was published after ChatGPT's training cutoff. Use the free AI Readability Checker to identify your specific issues.

What schema markup do I need for AI search?

For AI search visibility, your website needs: Organization schema (tells AI who you are), FAQPage schema (makes your content answerable), Service schema (defines what you offer), HowTo schema (for process content), and WebSite schema with SearchAction. FAQPage schema is the most important for appearing in Google AI Overviews and Perplexity citations.

What is the difference between SEO and GEO?

Traditional SEO optimizes for ranking in blue link results where users click through to your site. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) optimizes for being cited in AI-generated answers where AI summarizes your content directly. SEO success = clicks. GEO success = citations. In 2026, both are required as Google AI Mode handles 30%+ of all searches.

How does Perplexity decide which websites to cite?

Perplexity cites websites that clearly answer the query being searched, have high domain authority, allow PerplexityBot in robots.txt, contain passage-optimized answer blocks of 100-200 words, have proper schema markup, and demonstrate E-E-A-T signals. Perplexity heavily favors pages that answer one specific question comprehensively rather than covering many topics broadly.

What is E-E-A-T and why does it matter for AI?

E-E-A-T stands for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness and Trustworthiness — Google's framework for evaluating content quality. For AI search, E-E-A-T matters because AI systems like Gemini and Perplexity prioritize citing sources that demonstrate real-world experience, named expert authors, authoritative backlinks, and trust signals like contact information, location data and verified business information.

Does robots.txt affect AI visibility?

Yes. robots.txt directly controls whether AI crawlers can access your website. OpenAI's GPTBot, Anthropic's ClaudeBot, Perplexity's PerplexityBot, and Google's Google-Extended all respect robots.txt directives. If these bots are blocked or if your robots.txt has a blanket Disallow: /, AI systems cannot crawl your content and will not cite your website in their answers.

Which AI systems check robots.txt?

All major AI crawlers respect robots.txt directives: OpenAI uses GPTBot and OAI-SearchBot, Anthropic uses ClaudeBot and anthropic-ai, Perplexity uses PerplexityBot, Google uses Google-Extended for Gemini and AI Overviews, Meta uses meta-externalagent, Apple uses Applebot-Extended, Cohere uses cohere-ai, and Amazon uses Amazonbot. How does AI decide what to cite?

AI systems decide what to cite based on: relevance to the query, directness of the answer, source authority and E-E-A-T signals, structured data clarity, passage extractability, recency of content, and domain authority signals. Pages that answer one specific question directly and completely in 100-200 words are significantly more likely to be cited than long-form pages that cover a topic broadly.

What is passage indexing and why does it matter for AI?

Passage indexing is Google's ability to index and rank individual paragraphs within a page, not just the page as a whole. For AI search, passage indexing means AI systems can extract and cite a single well-written paragraph from your page even if the overall page isn't highly ranked. Writing self-contained 100-200 word answer blocks dramatically increases the chance of AI citation.
