The Challenge

The logistics network was buckling under scale, attempting to manually route thousands of packages. Dispatchers were overwhelmed, drivers were taking inefficient routes, and package tracking was fundamentally broken, leading to massive customer service backlogs.

Operating a national logistics network in Pakistan requires managing immense volatility. The client was reliant on legacy, on-premise software that was buckling under the exponential increase in e-commerce shipment volume. Dispatch routing was largely manual, relying on the geographic memory of seasoned operators rather than algorithmic optimization. This resulted in highly inefficient delivery routes, excess fuel consumption, and an unacceptable percentage of missed delivery windows. The absence of a robust API layer meant that integrating with major e-commerce platforms required manual data ingestion, which inherently bottlenecked scaling capabilities.

Our architectural solution centered on decoupling the Transport Management System (TMS) from the Warehouse Management System (WMS) utilizing a microservices approach. This allowed us to scale the tracking and routing engines independently during peak load periods. By integrating 12 distinct carrier APIs via Node.js middleware, the platform now automatically routes shipments based on real-time cost analysis and carrier performance metrics. The deployment of a native mobile application equipped with turn-by-turn navigation and digital proof-of-delivery completely digitized the last mile.

The Solution

We developed a high-throughput Transport Management System (TMS) and Warehouse Management System (WMS). We integrated 12 distinct carrier APIs and deployed a real-time GPS tracking mobile app for the entire fleet, enabling automated, optimized dispatch routing.

The Results

The platform immediately stabilized operations, seamlessly processing over 10,000 shipments daily. Algorithmic routing reduced average delivery times by 40%. Order accuracy skyrocketed to 99.2%, drastically reducing customer complaints.

The velocity of data flow through the new system transformed the operational paradigm. Dispatchers transitioned from manual routing to managing algorithmic exceptions. The real-time GPS tracking significantly reduced customer support call volume, as clients were empowered with accurate, live tracking portals. The aggregation of granular delivery data allowed the executive team to identify systemic bottlenecks at specific regional hubs, leading to targeted process optimizations. Ultimately, the technological foundation provided by MIRAC enabled the network to confidently onboard massive enterprise contracts that their previous infrastructure could not have supported.
